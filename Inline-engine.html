On this page I will show you how an inline engine works!

Introduction to Inline Engine:

  • An inline engine is a type of internal combustion engine where the cylinders are arranged in a straight line.
  • It is also known as a straight engine.

Cylinder Arrangement:

  • The cylinders are aligned in a single row along the engine block.

Combustion Process:

  • The engine operates on the principle of internal combustion, where fuel and air are mixed, compressed, and ignited.

Intake Stroke:

  • The intake valve opens, allowing a mixture of air and fuel to enter the cylinder.
  • The piston moves downward, creating a vacuum that draws in the fuel-air mixture.

Compression Stroke:

  • The intake and exhaust valves close.
  • The piston moves upward, compressing the fuel-air mixture in the cylinder.

Power Stroke:

  • The spark plug ignites the compressed fuel-air mixture.
  • The rapid expansion of gases forces the piston down, turning the crankshaft.

Exhaust Stroke:

  • The exhaust valve opens.
  • The piston moves upward, pushing the burned gases out of the cylinder.

Repeat Cycle:

  • The four-stroke cycle (intake, compression, power, exhaust) repeats for each cylinder in a continuous sequence.

Crankshaft and Connecting Rods:

  • The reciprocating motion of the pistons is converted into rotary motion by the crankshaft.
  • Connecting rods link the pistons to the crankshaft, transferring the linear motion.

Engine Balance:

  • Balancing mechanisms, such as counterweights on the crankshaft, are employed to minimize vibrations and ensure smooth operation.

Cooling System:

  • Inline engines typically have a cooling system, such as a radiator, to dissipate the heat generated during combustion.

Fuel Injection or Carburetion:

  • Modern inline engines often use fuel injection systems to precisely deliver fuel, replacing older carburetor systems.

Ignition System:

  • The ignition system, usually involving spark plugs, ignites the fuel-air mixture at the right moment in the combustion cycle.

Transmission Connection:

  • The output from the crankshaft is transmitted to the vehicle's transmission, which ultimately drives the wheels.
